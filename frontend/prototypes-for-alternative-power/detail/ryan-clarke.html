Shirley Sound
2021
Single-channel video, two-channel audio, custom interface


Kodak’s Shirley card used a portrait of a white woman to calibrate colour film printing from the 1940s to the 1990s. This supposedly neutral benchmark introduced a non-neutral standardisation bias, resulting in poor exposure for darker skin tones. The MPEG-1 Audio Layer III (MP3) codec, developed by German audio engineers using a Swedish state radio archive as its primary source, compresses sound according to a similarly biased standard.

Shirley Sound is Clarke’s reflection on what is lost in Black sound during digital compression. The video features prolific Black musicians such as Alice Coltrane and Milford Graves, collaged with viral videos and other materials personally significant to the artist. Using a simple crossfader, a common tool in music production, listeners hear two audio channels. The first features uncompressed sound, while the second highlights the part of the audio that is lost when the sound is compressed using the MP3 codec. Produced using the acoustic methodology of the late Ryan Patrick McGuire, Ph.D., the latter channel allows the listener to hear what the MP3 erases.
