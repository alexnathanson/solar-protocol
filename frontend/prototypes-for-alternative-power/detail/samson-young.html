Liquid Borders

Audio, images


Liquid Borders documents a physical intervention into the border infrastructure that separates Hong Kong and Shenzhen. In Hong Kong, a restricted area called the Frontier Closed Area (FCA) was established in 1951 as a buffer zone between the two cities. By 2012, large swathes of the FCA were unrestricted, and Young visited these areas to record sounds. Using contact microphones and hydrophones, he captured vibrations from fence wires and the flow of the Shenzhen River. These recordings were used to create sonic compositions that use movement to reveal the limits of border infrastructure designed to enforce separation.
